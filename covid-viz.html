Project

Covid-19 Data Visualization for AIIMS Delhi

Data Visualization · Medical Analytics

Overview

During my internship at Meiiporul Solutions Pvt. Ltd. in collaboration with AIIMS Delhi, I worked on a Covid-19 data visualization project to help clinicians and decision-makers better understand trends in pneumonia and Covid-affected lung X-rays. The goal was to build a MongoDB + Grafana-based analytics layer that surfaced actionable insights rather than raw spreadsheets.

My responsibilities included cleaning and structuring the data, designing a suitable data model, and building dashboards that highlighted case distributions, trends, and key differences between pneumonia and Covid-19 patients.

Context & goals

Covid-19 created an urgent need for reliable, real-time situational awareness inside hospitals. Doctors and administrators needed quick answers to questions like:

  • How many patients show Covid-typical vs pneumonia-typical patterns?
  • How is the case load evolving over time across different wards?
  • Where are we approaching capacity constraints for beds or equipment?

Our objective was not to replace clinical judgement, but to provide clear, visual summaries to support it.

Data & pipeline

I worked with anonymized, hospital-provided data containing information such as patient identifiers (de-identified), diagnosis labels (Covid vs pneumonia), timestamps, and imaging-related metadata. My contributions focused on:

  • Cleaning raw CSV/JSON files and standardizing field names and formats.
  • Designing a MongoDB schema suitable for time-series and cohort analysis.
  • Writing ETL scripts to load and update records in MongoDB collections.
  • Creating indices and query patterns optimized for dashboard filters.

Dashboards & visualizations

On top of the MongoDB backend, I built Grafana dashboards tailored to the needs of clinicians and hospital administrators:

  • Time-series charts tracking daily and weekly trends of Covid-typical versus pneumonia-typical cases.
  • Heatmaps and distribution plots showing case counts across age groups, wards, and other relevant categories.
  • Comparative panels that contrasted pneumonia and Covid-19 patterns to help identify shifts in disease burden.

These dashboards turned large, noisy tables into interactive visual stories that could be explored with simple filters and drill-downs.

Impact

The visualization layer made it easier for stakeholders at AIIMS Delhi to:

  • Monitor how Covid-19 and pneumonia cases were evolving over time.
  • Quickly identify spikes and potential stress points in the system.
  • Use evidence from dashboards to inform resource planning and logistics.

Although this was an intern project, it gave me practical experience turning raw medical data into dashboards that support decisions in a real hospital setting.
