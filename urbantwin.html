Project

UrbanTwin AI – Generative City Simulation

Graph Neural Networks · Geospatial ML · Climate & Urban Intelligence

Overview

UrbanTwin AI is an AI-powered digital twin of city regions built during UBHackopoly 2025. It simulates how changes in building density and green cover affect urban heat, traffic congestion, and air quality (PM2.5) using OpenStreetMap and MODIS data.

The system combines Graph Neural Networks (STGNNs), gradient-boosted models (LightGBM), and Google Vertex AI (Gemini 2.5 Pro) to power interactive, scenario-driven analysis through a web dashboard.

Motivation

City planners and communities need tools that make climate and infrastructure trade-offs easier to understand. UrbanTwin AI aims to answer questions like:

  • “What happens to urban heat if we add tree cover in this corridor?”
  • “How would densifying this block affect congestion and air quality?”

Data & graph construction

We start by constructing a city graph where each node represents a spatial unit (e.g., grid cell or building cluster) enriched with:

  • OpenStreetMap features (roads, buildings, land use, green spaces).
  • Remote-sensing signals (MODIS land-surface temperature, vegetation indices).
  • Traffic and population proxies where available.

Edges capture spatial adjacency and road connectivity, enabling spatio-temporal modeling of how heat and congestion propagate.

Modeling approach

  • STGNN module: learns patterns in time-varying environmental signals (e.g., heat, traffic) over the city graph.
  • LightGBM: models tabular relationships between features (built density, tree cover, road length) and target metrics like PM2.5.
  • Gemini 2.5 Pro: generates natural-language summaries of scenario outcomes, explaining changes for non-technical users.

Scenario simulation

Through the dashboard, users can edit a city region (e.g., increase green cover, adjust building heights) and run a “what if” simulation:

  1. Updated inputs are applied to the city graph and tabular features.
  2. STGNN + LightGBM recompute predicted heat, traffic, and PM2.5 metrics.
  3. Gemini summarizes the differences as a concise narrative and highlights key regions on the map.

Hackathon outcome

  • Delivered an end-to-end prototype in a weekend: data ingestion, modeling, and interactive UI.
  • Showcased how graph ML + LLMs can make complex environmental simulations accessible to city stakeholders.
